# UI/UX specification

## Principles

- Design for a narrow Obsidian sidebar first while scaling into a main pane.
- Use Obsidian theme variables and progressive disclosure.
- Never communicate state by color alone.
- Provide complete mouse, keyboard, touch, and trackpad paths.

## Navigation

The main-calendar title row groups previous period, the current-period entry, and next period into a compact leading navigation cluster. Direction buttons stay `6px` from the period entry, while the year/month or week-year/week triggers inside that entry use a `2px` gap. Today or This month stays at the trailing edge, separated by flexible space with a `12px` minimum. Month view shows separate year and month triggers, Week view shows separate ISO week-year and week triggers, and Year view shows only the year trigger. Month and Week use Today; Year uses This month, which returns to the current year and selects the current month at month granularity. Week navigation no longer opens the mini calendar: choosing a week-year or week stays in Week view and preserves the selected weekday, clamping W53 to the last valid week when necessary. The view switch belongs in the secondary toolbar. Month always keeps exactly one visible date in its roving Tab order; week/month or cross-query selection cannot remove that stop, and hidden outside-month heatmap cells do not participate. Arrow keys move focus through the displayed month's boundary-week grid and update the displayed month at a boundary. From a focused week number, ArrowRight moves into that week's first enabled date and changes to day-granularity selection.

Recognized periodic Markdown notes show a compact Note Navbar below the native view header: previous period, a calendar-icon period label, next period, open Chrono Notes, and the next genuinely openable higher period. Previous/date/next and the optional higher-period command form one centered navigation group with clear separation between peer and hierarchy navigation; open-calendar remains alone in the trailing action area. The period label opens the same mini calendar and selection opens that periodic-note type. If an adjacent higher type is disabled, has an empty pattern, or cannot recognize its own generated path, the command continues to the next valid type; no command is rendered when none exists. Narrow editors hide only the higher-period text while retaining its icon button, ARIA label, and title. Each open periodic Markdown leaf owns its Navbar; moving focus to a sidebar must not unmount it, and split periodic-note panes keep independent instances. Mobile follows system safe area → native view header → Navbar → editor content: the Navbar remains outside the editor's scrolling region and occupies real layout height, floating-navigation and auto-full-screen phone modes reserve the host header exactly once, and ordinary phones, tablets, and desktop gain no extra blank band. Expanding related ranges only reduces the editor's scrollable height and never overlays host controls or content. Weekly and monthly notes can expand up to five range notes overlapping the period, open them in new tabs, show `+N` overflow, and persist collapse state. Ordinary notes and disabled or partial path matches mount no Navbar.

The command palette exposes both the mini calendar and direct date jump. Direct input retains legacy `yyyy-MM-dd`, `yyyy/MM/dd`, `yyyy.MM.dd`, and `yyyyMMdd` formats. Invalid dates never roll into an adjacent month; a successful jump opens the main month view and selects the target date.

The mini calendar uses the same layout grammar: previous month, a nominally `132px` year/month trigger that may shrink at narrow widths, and next month form a compact leading navigation cluster with `6px` internal gaps; Today stays at the trailing edge, with at least `12px` of flexible space between the two groups and no duplicate footer action. The date model still supplies only the four to six complete boundary weeks intersecting the target month, while presentation reserves six fixed-height row tracks to keep the modal dimensions stable without generating, displaying, or exposing wholly unrelated placeholder weeks. Changing months must not move title-row controls.

```text
Main · Month  [‹] [2026⌄][Sep⌄] [›] ───── [Today]
Main · Week   [‹] [2026⌄][W40⌄] [›] ───── [Today]
Main · Year   [‹] [2026⌄] [›] ─────────── [This month]
Mini          [‹] [September 2026⌄] [›] ─ [Today]
```

Heatmap and view-specific actions belong in a secondary toolbar. Month and Year keep independent heatmap toggles but, when enabled, share the statistic dimension, thresholds, 0–4 legend, and accessible semantics. When disabled, only the flame remains. Week renders neither heatmap controls nor a disabled placeholder. At narrow widths the legend disappears first; if space is still insufficient, the intact dimension-and-flame group wraps to a right-aligned second row without shrinking text or splitting controls.

The secondary-toolbar-to-content rhythm is view-specific. Month retains its existing `4px` toolbar bottom spacing, `24px` weekday header, and `6px` gap from that header to the date grid. Week and Year have no weekday header, so they keep `8px` of effective space from the bottom edge of the complete toolbar—including a wrapped second row at narrow widths—to the first main-content cell or section. This spacing must not be produced with an empty or simulated weekday header, nor by changing the internal padding, gap, or height of day cells, period entries, or heatmap cells.

The title/date entry must provide efficient target-year, target-month, ISO week-year, and week selection plus actions to open the corresponding Gregorian yearly, quarterly, or monthly note. An accessible popover, modal, or staged picker may replace the legacy fixed 20-year and quarter table, but keyboard use, new-tab opening, and mutual exclusion between open pickers remain available. A week-year dynamically contains 52 or 53 weeks and must never be hard-coded to 52.

A flame icon button with `aria-pressed` toggles the month heatmap. Enabling it also shows the shared statistic dimension and a 0–4 legend that uses the same accent palette as month cells. In heatmap mode, cells keep only a date number centered both horizontally and vertically plus four theme-accent intensity levels, and no range Gantt is rendered. Outside-month cells retain grid space but hide content and leave the interaction model. Dimension, raw value, and level remain in each date's accessible name and hover/focus preview. The heat level always owns the data background; today adds a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge, selection adds an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, and focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection. None replaces the data background or changes text color, font weight, or decoration. Returning to normal mode restores the existing selection, opening, and date-metadata presentation.

## Month cells

The top accessory row spans the cell's entire usable width. The state dot and task progress share a fixed left anchor, while the regional marker uses a fixed right anchor. Task progress grows only rightward from that anchor; the two sides retain at least `4px` of separation, and a narrow cell shortens progress rather than moving either anchor or allowing overlap. If an `8px` state plus the required gap leaves insufficient room for a multi-character marker, only the marker text may ellipsize; its complete meaning remains in the tooltip and accessible label.

Information priority is a fixed top row with note state or horizontal task progress on the left and one regional marker on the right, a full-width centered date row, one or two calendar extensions, regional-holiday names, then ICS summaries. The date keeps `3px` of effective spacing before the following details. The top row retains stable height when either side is empty, and the date starts below both accessories without absolute offsets or combination-specific translation. Regional markers use compact plain text with no border or badge background. Remaining items follow one vertical content track instead of exchanging order through absolute footers. One extension is centered. Two use equal inline columns when a cell is about `60px` or wider and stack in a narrow calendar. Single and dual modes use the same type size, with no hiding, shrinking, or horizontal scrolling. Each extension retains both its structural date and optional event, so a solar term or festival cannot erase a month transition. Lower-priority overflow collapses into a count and remains available by hover, focus, or details UI. A normal month cell keeps a further-tightened `52px` shared base minimum and reserves no space for holiday or ICS content that is not present. Regional-holiday names and ICS occupy only their actual visible auto rows; when visible content needs more space, only that week's seven date cells and week number grow uniformly, without affecting other weeks in the month. Leading and trailing outside-month dates use a quieter whole-cell background, border, and content treatment. Today uses a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge, selection uses an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, and focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection; today never reuses the note-state dot. Current, selected, and focus do not change background, text color, font weight, or decoration. Today, selection, range preview, hover, and keyboard focus restore full contrast, and content opacity must never fade the focus outline.

The left week number is a weekly-note entry rather than a static label. Each row uses a compact `W` prefix and tabular week number, such as `W27`, while the column header remains localized as Week/周/週. The month main grid uses a `6px` gap by default and `4px` at `≤360px`; an additional stable spacer separates the transparent week-index rail from the seven date columns. It uses no full-height divider and cannot read as an eighth date cell. Each week-number entry is an independent cell with the same `5px` rounding on all four corners as a date cell, not the square trailing corners inherited from a continuous rail. Click selects the week; double-click, Enter, or long press opens its weekly note by default; middle-click or `Ctrl / Cmd`-click opens a tab. Because this rail has no regional accessory, its state dot or horizontal progress is an absolute top overlay and the `W` label remains centered against the complete control. The week containing local today uses `is-current-period`, a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge, and `aria-current="true"`; a selected week adds an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary. Both may coexist, focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection, and none changes background, text color, font weight, or decoration. Disabled weekly notes, month heatmap, and the Show note status toggle omit the status overlay entirely without reserving an empty row; current/selected state, the weekly path, and complete accessible text remain. A narrow sidebar must still fit `W53` and its indicator.

At regular widths, ICS shows at most three stably ordered summaries per date cell. A starting segment shows all-day or local start time, continuation uses leading or trailing ellipses, and additional events collapse into `+N`. The cell's accessible name retains every title, source, all-day/time value, and continuation state, including folded events. Month heatmap, narrow-container, and coarse-pointer layouts may hide the visible summaries without changing that accessible information.

Chinese lunar uses traditional month/day text in Chinese locales. In English it uses compact numeric `Lunar M/D` text, including an explicit leap-month marker, plus English solar-term and festival names. Ganzhi shows Chinese pillars in Chinese locales and pinyin pillars in English; it shows the day pillar normally and the new month pillar on a solar-term transition. Complete localized calendar text stays in the cell's accessible name, and the extension tooltip retains the full lunar month/day or Ganzhi year/month/day pillars. Calendar extensions provide calendar information only, never fortune-telling content.
The top-right regional slot follows the resolved plugin language: Simplified and Traditional Chinese use `班 / 休 / 公假`, while English uses `Work / Off / PH`. Color is only a secondary cue. Holiday regions use three neutral ordered Region 1/2/3 slots. Adjusted-workday markers always win; other same-day markers follow slot order, and exactly one visible marker may exist in the DOM. Holiday names sit below calendar extensions and above ICS; configured regions form separate centered lines, while names in one region share a line. Holidays that lose the visible-marker priority remain complete in accessible labels and hover/focus previews.

A read-only note preview appears after hover or keyboard focus remains for 250 ms and is cancelled immediately on leave or blur. The preview avoids covering its date cell, stays within the viewport, and repositions on scroll or resize. Empty, YAML-only, non-previewable body, and read-error states use explicit text. Disabling hover previews immediately cancels both pending timers and mounted tooltips and must not create another read path or background request. Year-day previews belong only to heatmap mode: switching to summary, changing the displayed year, or disabling previews cancels the timer, unmounts the tooltip, and clears its ARIA relationship without a stale-tooltip flash.

## State and interaction

The main calendar uses `is-current-period` consistently for an exact date or period containing local today, with current, selected, and keyboard focus treated as orthogonal states. Dates, week numbers, and year-summary month/quarter entries represent current with a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge. Selected uses an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, while keyboard focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection. All three may coexist without changing background, text color, font weight, or decoration. Grid gaps must reserve real space for the selected frame; an inset or inward-offset imitation is not acceptable. Exact dates use `aria-current="date"`; week, month, and quarter period buttons use `aria-current="true"`; selection retains the appropriate `aria-selected` or `aria-pressed`. Temporary states such as month range preview/endpoints and week task drop targets take priority, while selected and focus remain independent. Month and year heatmaps retain their data-level backgrounds and reuse the same current/selected/focus frame matrix. Native buttons and selects in Calendar, Note Navbar, and the range list must keep a visible `:focus-visible` outer frame under host themes; date cells retain their own outermost focus ring without adding a duplicate. This main-grid contract is not applied directly to the period picker or mini calendar. The period picker independently represents current with accent text plus `aria-current` and no dot; selected adds only a `2px` inset outline plus `aria-pressed`, without changing text color or background; focus uses a separate outer outline, and current may coexist with selected. The mini calendar retains its existing state styling.

Indexing, missing, empty, YAML-only, body, tasks, and read-error states use shape plus color and readable tooltips. Indexing uses a muted dashed hollow dot; missing alone uses the error-color hollow dot. Note open/create actions are omitted from a date context menu while that path remains unknown, and direct periodic-note commands show a localized retry-later notice instead of creating. Click selects; double-click or long press opens; Ctrl/Cmd-click or middle click opens a tab; right click opens context actions; arrows move; Enter opens; Escape dismisses or cancels.

Touch long press uses a 500 ms threshold. Touch move, end, or cancel before the threshold cancels opening. A successful long press suppresses exactly one synthetic browser click so selection cannot toggle and the note cannot open twice; unmounting clears pending work.

On desktop, an unmodified primary-button press in month view establishes a range anchor. Entering another date cell previews the normalized range, including reverse drags. Creation is submitted only after at least two distinct dates are crossed and the primary button is released. A same-cell press/release remains ordinary selection; a completed drag consumes the next click; Escape or invalid drag termination clears only the preview. Creation goes through the existing range command and confirmation chain, never a direct Vault write from React.

The date context menu's Create range note action is independent of daily-note configuration and appears only when the creation folder is non-empty. Its input modal defaults to the selected date and the following day and accepts two distinct `YYYY-MM-DD` values in either order. With range confirmation enabled, Continue then shows the normalized range, inclusive day count, and target path for confirmation; disabling confirmation skips only that step and preserves date entry. An existing note at that path opens directly without confirmation or overwrite. “Do not confirm future range-note creation” is cleared by default; only selecting it and confirming creation disables later confirmations for that category. Cancel, close, and Escape never change settings.

Normal Month and Week views share one simplified Gantt component; Month heatmap renders no range Gantt. A range bar spans its start and end date columns instead of appearing as a standalone date-range cell; overlapping ranges move to another lane and non-overlapping ranges reuse a lane. Normal Month layout assigns lanes once across the complete visible four-to-six-week window, so cross-week segments retain vertical position and a stable identity color. Every month week block is a two-track grid containing a date-content track and a range track. The backgrounds and borders of the week number, spacer, and seven date buttons span both tracks, while date information occupies only the first track. The range Gantt participates in normal flow on the second track, after ICS and before the date buttons' bottom borders. With no ranges, the second track collapses to `0`; multiple lanes grow only their own week and must not force every other week in the month to share that height. An absolute overlay, estimated bottom reservation, or parallel height function must not simulate this space. Under a fine pointer, every visible month lane is a separate, non-overlapping `10px` interactive row with a `4px` visual line vertically centered inside, `0` inter-lane gap, and a `2px` strip bottom inset. Under a coarse pointer, only the interactive row expands to `18px`; the visual line remains `4px`. There is no additional transparent hit layer, adjacent lanes may not share hit regions, and ICS may not overlap a line. In both month and week views, interactive range bars remain independent buttons outside day buttons. The month Gantt container is a sibling of the day-button row, while the week Gantt occupies a normal-flow sibling region after the seven day buttons; neither may nest interactive controls or duplicate the range list. Month view hides the in-bar title and retains identity through color, tooltip, and ARIA, while week view keeps the in-bar title when space permits. Ellipses indicate continuation. Eight categorical colors adjust their lightness toward the current theme foreground so they remain distinct from the date-cell background without hover; hover and focus must be clear but remain supplemental feedback. All visible cross-week segments for the same canonical note path share a linked state in month view: hovering or keyboard-focusing any segment highlights the others, while the originating segment retains stronger feedback; pointer exit or blur clears the corresponding linked state. Association is by canonical path only, never by a shared color index or lane number. Linked and originating-segment feedback may change color, outline, or stacking only, without changing row height, gap, line width, position, column span, or hit region. Colors identify notes without reusing success, warning, or error semantics and never replace the month tooltip/ARIA or the week in-bar title. Overflow beyond the lane limit becomes `+N`, with hidden titles available on hover or focus. The complete tooltip includes title, source date/time range, and inclusive day count, and a click opens the note in a new tab.

Week, month, and year share one segmented view control. Week uses separate week-year and week triggers instead of the former two-line title and mini-calendar action. The week-year trigger reuses the 20-year window; the week trigger lists every valid week chronologically. Its ideal popup width is `360–420px`, using three weeks per row from `360px` and two below that. Each week is one native `44px` button/focus target with two internal columns, such as `W01 | 12/29–1/4`; a same-month range contracts to `7/20–26`, while tooltip and ARIA retain both complete dates and years. The popup scrolls vertically and reveals the selected week on open. Across year, month, ISO week-year, and week-number popovers, current uses accent text plus `aria-current`, with no dot or weight change; selected uses only a `2px` inset outline plus `aria-pressed`, without changing text color or background; focus uses a separate outer outline, and current may coexist with selected. A month is current only when the popover is browsing the current year, and a week is current only when the browsed ISO week-year matches the current week-year. Arrow keys follow the visual grid, Home/End reach the endpoints, Page Up/Down move five rows, and one or two typed digits locate a week. Previous/next moves exactly seven days and preserves the selected weekday. The seven-day overview reuses the month cell's complete `CalendarDay` hierarchy: row one contains note state or horizontal task progress plus the work/rest or regional marker, row two contains the weekday, followed by date, calendar extensions, holidays, and ICS. The Week main grid uses a `6px` gap by default and `4px` at `≤360px`; the Week Gantt uses the same `6px / 4px` column gaps for exact alignment, while the date keeps `3px` of effective spacing before details. Assistive technology receives a labelled selection group of native day buttons: each uses `aria-pressed` for selection, while today independently uses `aria-current="date"`. It is not exposed as an ARIA grid because it has neither grid rows nor roving focus. Wide density shows the full weekday and up to three ICS summaries, medium density shows the short weekday and one ICS summary, and narrow density shows the narrowest weekday/date while folding details; complete tooltip and ARIA remain in all tiers. Today uses a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge, selection uses an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, and focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection; none changes background, text color, font weight, or decoration, while temporary task drop targets take priority. Click selects, double-click or Enter opens by default, and middle-click or Ctrl/Cmd opens a tab. A configured weekly note has its own compact open/create row. With no ranges, the view shows no redundant empty paragraph and keeps only a compact week-start create action when the creation folder is valid. With no tasks, the heading's zero count is the empty state and no second explanatory paragraph appears.

The week task list aggregates Vault-wide tasks with due, scheduled, or start dates, deduplicating the same task on the same date and naming the matching date types. Only an incomplete task's due date produces overdue state: one or two days is a warning and three or more is severe. Completed tasks and scheduled/start-only tasks are not overdue. An overdue row must show visible "overdue" or "seriously overdue" level text, and the checkbox accessible name retains both the date types and level. Color is supplementary; a narrow sidebar may hide ordinary date types but not the overdue level. The checkbox toggles through a command, while task text opens a new tab at the exact source line. Only the occurrence grouped under its own due date can be rescheduled: desktop users may drag it to another visible day, and the row also exposes a native seven-day select with localized dates and the complete task name for keyboard and touch use. Scheduled/start duplicates expose neither path. Both wait for Vault events to refresh and never edit files directly.

The range-note list opens from the command palette or the Range notes settings tab. Search matches titles and paths case-insensitively. Scope can show all notes, ranges overlapping the current month, or ranges overlapping the current year. Sorting is ascending or descending by start date, with end date, title, and path as deterministic tie breakers. The toolbar shows the result count and a create action; an empty index and no search matches have distinct states. Clicking an item opens it in a new tab. The list refreshes immediately after Vault create/modify/rename/delete events or scan-setting changes, and its toolbar stacks on narrow screens without truncating paths.

After the plugin is enabled for the first time and the workspace layout is ready, it shows one short guide. The seen marker is persisted before opening the modal so reloads or multiple layout callbacks cannot repeat it. Dismiss, Open Settings, and Escape all count as seen. General settings always provides a Show Again action; reopening manually does not reset the marker.

The guide only describes real, easy-to-miss interactions: click selects a date or period; double-click or Enter opens by default; middle-click or Ctrl/Cmd-click opens a tab; touch can long-press; date context actions cover notes, ranges, and copying; cell shapes, numbers, and text express note, task, and calendar state; and the Note Navbar moves between or up periodic-note levels. It uses a native Obsidian modal with Open Settings and Got it actions, not a marketing page or multi-step overlay tour.

## Settings and responsive behavior

Holiday regions in Appearance & views use three neutral ordered Region 1/2/3 dropdown slots. They list only implemented providers, omit choices already used by another slot, and compact after an earlier slot is cleared.

Settings use five horizontally scrollable tabs: General, Appearance & views, Periodic notes, Range notes, and Integrations. Tabs implement `tablist/tab/tabpanel`, arrow keys, Home, and End. The active tab scrolls into view after click activation, keyboard activation, or settings rerendering. The tab list hides vertical overflow so it never shows a thin vertical scrollbar. Tab height is 34px for desktop fine pointers and 44px for coarse pointers. Disabled dependencies explain why they are inactive.

Language offers Auto, English, Simplified Chinese, and Traditional Chinese. Open UI refreshes immediately after a change. Product copy, tooltips, and ARIA use one message catalog; all three catalogs must completely cover the same key set and interpolation placeholders. Dates, months, weekdays, and numbers use `Intl` with the resolved locale. Text-width changes must not overflow buttons or shift fixed controls.

Appearance & views provides font sizing that follows Obsidian, follows the sidebar, or uses a fixed factor; two ordered calendar-extension slots; a Show note status toggle; quarter labels as localized numeric names or `春 / 夏 / 秋 / 冬`; and task annotation as none, color, or hollow. Each extension slot is a dropdown that omits the provider already selected by the other slot; clearing the first compacts the second forward. Fixed mode exposes a 0–20 slider with a default of 10; other modes preserve its value while disabling the slider. Sidebar following uses discrete container buckets, never continuous viewport-width scaling. Task annotation defaults to hollow: no-task cells retain note state and completed progress uses the success color; unfinished color mode uses a warning-filled track, while unfinished hollow mode uses a warning hollow track. None falls back only the visible task progress to the note-state symbol and must not remove task statistics, heatmap or preview information, the task list, or ARIA. Quarter-label changes immediately update year-summary labels, year-heatmap quarter headings, and the month picker without changing quarter anchors, note paths, or Note Navbar labels. With Show note status enabled, month, week, and year use only a fixed top state dot or proportional horizontal progress track. Completed and total counts remain in ARIA, tooltip, and indicator data rather than occupying the cell as fraction text. Disabling the toggle unmounts visible dots and progress without removing note and task state. General provides an “Open date properties with Chrono Notes” toggle, disabled by default. It captures only the open icon beside a date property when daily notes are configured. An ordinary click uses the default open target, while `Ctrl / Cmd` opens a new tab; Wiki/Markdown links in Properties, body links, and every other control retain Obsidian's behavior.

The Periodic notes tab presents the five note types as independent collapsible sections. A disabled type shows only its label and enable toggle; enabling it reveals the path pattern and template path. Its behavior section has an independent Confirm periodic-note creation toggle. “Do not confirm future periodic-note creation” is cleared by default; only selecting it and confirming creation disables later confirmation. Cancel, close, and Escape do not change the setting, and larger-period cascades do not prompt again. Shared guidance before the five sections always explains that the pattern contains both the folder and filename, fixed Latin-letter text and similar token-like literals use Luxon single quotes while pure Chinese text may be written directly, a root-level note needs only the filename pattern, and Obsidian's `YYYY-MM-DD` becomes Luxon's `yyyy-MM-dd`. Every path input uses a period-specific complete path, including a folder, as its placeholder and repeats that example in a persistent line. Path-pattern and template controls always use one column: the label and guidance sit above a full-width input, independent of panel width. A valid path shows Current preview on its own line immediately below the input; empty values, common Moment tokens, and patterns that fail unified reverse recognition show their specific validation error in that same position. Errors use error color, `aria-invalid`, and a live accessible description. The input uses Obsidian's native keyboard suggestions for Vault folders, and the anchored suggestion popover matches the full input width. Choosing a folder preserves the current filename pattern, or supplies the canonical filename pattern for that period when the field is empty. The preview is the complete `.md` path generated for the current date and week-start rule. Template inputs have period-specific placeholders, suggest Markdown files only, and write complete Vault-relative paths; an empty value still means a blank note, and the field label does not depend on the selected template engine. Manual path input disables auto-capitalization, autocomplete, and spellcheck. Neither suggestions nor previews may create parallel configuration or read note bodies.

The Range notes tab includes an independent Confirm range-note creation toggle, calendar visibility, creation folder, scan scope (range folder, custom folder, or entire Vault), custom scan folder, and month/week display limits. The custom-folder input is enabled only for that scope. An empty creation folder removes creation entry points without inventing or deleting indexed range data.

The Integrations tab provides local-ICS visibility, one read-only source per line, manual refresh, and per-source status. A source can be a Vault file, a desktop-vault-relative path, or a desktop absolute path. Successful sources remain visible beside failures. Status explicitly reports event counts, skipped recurrence, and errors instead of silently treating recurrence as imported.

Density responds to container width. In summary mode, year view groups a quarter entry with its three month entries and uses a `6px` gap normally or `4px` at `≤360px`. This month changes to the current year, selects the current month through its `day: 1` month anchor, and mounts and scrolls the matching quarter into view. A visible quarter/month note indicator is an absolute top overlay anchored `4px` from the cell top, leaving about `2px` of visible separation from the text. The period label remains centered in the complete button, the entry height stays unchanged, and a disabled note type or hidden indicator mounts no empty status slot. Quarter and month entries containing local today use `is-current-period`, a neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge, and `aria-current="true"`; selection adds an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, while focus remains an independent outermost frame beyond selection. None changes background, text color, font weight, or decoration. The same centered-label and current/selected/focus rules apply to the indicator-free period buttons in heatmap mode. Heatmap mode shows three months of daily intensity cells per quarter, with `4px` gaps normally or `3px` at `≤360px`, and the statistic selector and 0–4 legend shared with Month view. Months, quarters, and days are selectable; double-click or Enter opens the default target, while middle-click or Ctrl/Cmd opens a tab. Each monthly grid exposes seven actual ARIA rows that own its daily gridcells. Because weekdays are rows and weeks are columns, Left/Right move `-7/+7` days and Up/Down move `-1/+1` day along the visual axes. Daily heatmap cells retain context menus, heatmap-only hover/focus previews, and non-color ARIA values. Today uses `aria-current="date"`, retains its heat-level background, and adds the neutral `1px` inner border flush with the cell's inner edge; selection adds an accent `2px` frame genuinely outside the cell boundary, while focus remains independent and outermost beyond selection. A rendered selected day from the displayed year is the only roving Tab stop; otherwise January 1 is the sole temporary fallback. The stop moves to the selected day when its quarter mounts, while a selection from another displayed year cannot leave the grid without a stop. Year rendering keeps only the current quarter window and adjacent overscan mounted, with placeholders matching the current responsive quarter height; returning to month view preserves the latest selected month context. Date cells never read files independently, and animation honors reduced-motion preferences.

When interval lanes overflow, `+N` is a real button with `aria-expanded`, `aria-controls`, and `aria-haspopup="dialog"`, not static tooltip-only text. It opens a current-week-only hidden-item panel. Every item retains its canonical path, title, original boundaries, inclusive duration, and task statistics, uses an independent target of at least `44px`, and keeps the existing open-in-tab action. The desktop panel is anchored to its Gantt; mobile keeps it inside system safe areas. Escape or an outside pointer action closes it, and Escape restores trigger focus.

Vault-path suggestions are capped at 100. An empty query takes the first 100 path-sorted candidates. A fuzzy query preserves Obsidian's all-candidate matching and ranking but retains only the best 100. One catalog is shared while settings are open, rebuilds lazily after create/delete/rename but not modify, and releases its listeners and data when settings close.
